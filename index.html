The Story of Us

We met on the last warm-ish day of 2020. Warm-ish for Boston anyway. Taryn wouldn't take her jacket off for the entire outdoor meal. By our second date we realized how much we had in common, our love for the great outdoors and an unhealthy obsession with yoga pants. By the end of the date, we had forgoed all social distancing recommendations and shared our first kiss. There was instant chemistry, and we couldn’t stop spending time together.

Next, we had our first COVID scare. Taryn skipped town for a few weeks, and Yoni discovered just how much he had fallen for her. Absence makes the heart grow fonder? Reunited at last, we were inseparable. We shared many nights cooking together, followed by elegant picnics on the floor in Yoni’s apartment. Really though, Yoni didn’t have any furniture for the first six months of us dating.

Since then, we’ve moved in together, rectified the furniture problem, adopted many plant babies, and spent a lot of time with our family and friends. In June of 2021, Taryn came home from school with the award for Best COVID Romance. We’ve taken many road trips together, in Taryn’s car obviously, as Yoni won’t let her in Stacy (his beloved first car).

In early October of 2021, Yoni popped the question and a bottle of Champagne while we were on our favorite local hike. We are absolutely buzzing, and cannot wait to celebrate with you at our wedding!

The Venue

Lighthouse Point Park is a park in New Haven, Connecticut. It is home to the deactivated Five Mile Point Light, which was built in 1847 out of East Haven brownstone. It is also home to the Lighthouse Point Carousel, the 1905-built carousel and the 1916-constructed building that are the site of our wedding.
